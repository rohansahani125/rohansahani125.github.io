Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA)

The BBA curriculum includes courses in areas such as accounting, finance, marketing, human resources, operations management, and organisational behaviour. The program is aimed at providing students with a well-rounded understanding of business and management and equipping them with the skills required to work effectively in a corporate environment. BBA graduates can pursue careers in a variety of fields, including finance, marketing, sales, human resources, and operations management. Many BBA graduates also go on to pursue higher education, such as an MBA, to further their careers. Eligibility Criteria for BBA

Before applying for the BBA course, students should check the eligibility criteria and ensure they meet all the requirements. The BBA eligibility criteria may include subjects required, educational qualification, age limit, etc. Candidates can review the eligibility criteria for the BBA course listed below.

  • Candidates must have passed class 12 with a minimum aggregate mark of 50% or equivalent from a recognized board.
  • Some universities may conduct entrance exams; candidates must check individual university requirements.

Note: Eligibility criteria may vary between universities, and it is essential to verify specific requirements with the respective institutions.

Skills Required for BBA

To succeed in the field of business administration, certain skills are crucial. Here are the essential skills required for pursuing a BBA:

  • Effective communication skills
  • Problem-solving abilities
  • Leadership and teamwork skills
  • Time management and organizational skills
  • Analytical and critical thinking skills
  • Adaptability to changing business environments
